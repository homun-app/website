Homun is a **personal AI that does the work — on your terms**. It runs on your machine
(a signed desktop app or a single container you self-host): it remembers what matters in
a memory graph you own, drafts across your channels, automates recurring work, and can
drive a real contained computer to get things done — with local and cloud models side by
side, under permissions you grant.

It's not a passive chat box. Today it assists you; the direction is an **apprentice** that
observes your work, learns your routines, proposes, and takes a task end-to-end. That
autonomous loop is [on the roadmap](/roadmap/).

## What makes it different

- **On your terms.** Memory, contacts and history live in plain SQLite + files under
  `~/.homun` — on your machine, or a server you run. You decide what's delegated to a
  cloud model, and when.
- **Local and cloud, side by side.** Point it at local [Ollama](https://ollama.com) or
  any cloud provider; a router picks the right model per task.
- **A real computer.** The agent can open a contained browser/shell and actually do
  things — and you can watch it live.
- **Transparent.** Memory is an inspectable graph + a Markdown wiki you can read and
  correct, not an opaque blob.
